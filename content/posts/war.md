# Introduction

<img src="/statue-carving-itself.webp" alt="Statue carving itself" style="width:50%">


> "You have to be willing to go to war with yourself and create a whole new identity." 
~ David Goggins

What’s a phoenix but a magnificent metaphor for self transformation? Yes, this is what the phoenix is for this post. To produce great works of art and minor works of art requires momentum and production.
Some people draw on a canvas, a philosopher loves knowledge and lives according to its dictates which necessarily requires self transformation and modification. The self is an illusion, but consciousness is very real. Consciousness is the operator on the mind’s state and thus changes the brain according to the will.
The will is hard to understand or define. To find your limit you must go past the comfortable and further still. Most people never reach their limit because they’ve never realized what they’re capable of. 
To go to war with oneself requires a depth of respect for yourself to say “I just need a little more” and go past that. You reach the limit when you either break or the wall breaks. In art grit matters a lot, people who can wake up again and again to pursue their goal.
In this post I am talking about art as a life choice.
An artist in life is someone who has decided to rage against the status quo in themselves and transform. I repeat to emphasize.
Entrepreneurs are similar to artists in that they way to say something with their work and they’re willing to go to war against the status quo to make it happen.


All artists are fighting an inner war. 
It is won or lost every night and renewed to try again each morning.

“That the spiritual man need not be a recluse, that union with the divine Life may be achieved and maintained in the midst of worldly affairs, that the obstacles to that union lie not outside us but within us—such is the central lesson of the Bhagavad-Gītā.” ~ Annie Besant, "The Bhagavad Gita: The Lord's Song", The Theosophical Publishing House, Adyar, Preface

> “Rule of art: Cant kills creativity!” ~ Camille Paglia

Do it, don’t make excuses.

> “Sometimes one’s very angry and preaches, but I know that to clinch a point is to close it. To leave the reader free to decide what your work means, that’s the real art; it makes the work inexhaustible.” 
~ Ursula K Le Guin

Living life as an artist is a practice.
You are either engaging in the practice
or you’re not.

It makes no sense to say you’re not good at it.
It’s like saying, “I’m not good at being a monk.”
You are either living as a monk or you’re not.
We tend to think of the artist’s work as the output.
The real work of the artist is a way of being in the world.
Rick Rubin, The Creative Act: A Way of Being
“All that matters is that you are making something you love, to the best of your ability, here and now.”
Rick Rubin, The Creative Act: A Way of Being
 
> “The society that separates its scholars from its warriors will have its thinking done by cowards and its fighting by fools.” ~ Thucydides

An artist warrior sounds like a fitting pair too.

> "Only by being able to endure what people cannot bear, can you do what people cannot do." - John D. Rockefeller's Letter to His Son

This is the way, but there are others. We do these things because they're hard
and growth is on the other side.


> "Art attracts us only by what it reveals of our most secret self."  ~Jean-Luc Godard

To go to war takes courage

> "The cowards never started and the weak died along the way. That leaves us." ~ Founder of Nike, Phil Knight
