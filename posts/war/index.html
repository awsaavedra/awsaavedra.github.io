Art as War

Published: 29 Mar, 2026

Introduction

“You have to be willing to go to war with yourself and create a whole new identity.” ~ David Goggins

What’s a phoenix but a magnificent metaphor for self transformation? Yes, this is what the phoenix is for this post. To produce great works of art and minor works of art requires momentum and production. Some people draw on a canvas, a philosopher loves knowledge and lives according to its dictates which necessarily requires self transformation and modification. The self is an illusion, but consciousness is very real. Consciousness is the operator on the mind’s state and thus changes the brain according to the will. The will is hard to understand or define. To find your limit you must go past the comfortable and further still. Most people never reach their limit because they’ve never realized what they’re capable of. To go to war with oneself requires a depth of respect for yourself to say “I just need a little more” and go past that. You reach the limit when you either break or the wall breaks. In art grit matters a lot, people who can wake up again and again to pursue their goal. In this post I am talking about art as a life choice. An artist in life is someone who has decided to rage against the status quo in themselves and transform. I repeat to emphasize. Entrepreneurs are similar to artists in that they way to say something with their work and they’re willing to go to war against the status quo to make it happen.

All artists are fighting an inner war. It is won or lost every night and renewed to try again each morning.

“That the spiritual man need not be a recluse, that union with the divine Life may be achieved and maintained in the midst of worldly affairs, that the obstacles to that union lie not outside us but within us—such is the central lesson of the Bhagavad-Gītā.” ~ Annie Besant, “The Bhagavad Gita: The Lord’s Song”, The Theosophical Publishing House, Adyar, Preface

“Rule of art: Cant kills creativity!” ~ Camille Paglia

Do it, don’t make excuses.

“Sometimes one’s very angry and preaches, but I know that to clinch a point is to close it. To leave the reader free to decide what your work means, that’s the real art; it makes the work inexhaustible.” ~ Ursula K Le Guin

Living life as an artist is a practice. You are either engaging in the practice or you’re not.

It makes no sense to say you’re not good at it. It’s like saying, “I’m not good at being a monk.” You are either living as a monk or you’re not. We tend to think of the artist’s work as the output. The real work of the artist is a way of being in the world. Rick Rubin, The Creative Act: A Way of Being “All that matters is that you are making something you love, to the best of your ability, here and now.” Rick Rubin, The Creative Act: A Way of Being

“The society that separates its scholars from its warriors will have its thinking done by cowards and its fighting by fools.” ~ Thucydides

An artist warrior sounds like a fitting pair too.

“Only by being able to endure what people cannot bear, can you do what people cannot do.” - John D. Rockefeller’s Letter to His Son

This is the way, but there are others. We do these things because they’re hard and growth is on the other side.

“Art attracts us only by what it reveals of our most secret self.” ~Jean-Luc Godard

To go to war takes courage

“The cowards never started and the weak died along the way. That leaves us.” ~ Founder of Nike, Phil Knight

You’re all that’s left, start the war with thyself.

Art is Authentic and True

“You moment you start believing you are a prophet, you stop being an artist.” “Art is about truth, not about becoming famous.” ~ Robert Smith, The Cure

In many ways I think Robert Smith is probably the only person to look towards for this because his art is earnest in ways that can even feel painful and silly to an outsider.

Truth and Beauty

“[repressive governments] produce bad art, produces social inertia, produces really unhappy people, and it’s more repressive than any other kind of government.” ~ Frank Zappa

It has been revealed to me that ugly works come from ugly feckless people who have disgusting taste.

A sense of taste matters more than raw ability in art because someone who has vision and taste knows what the top of the summit looks like.

The ability to powerful and vividly visualize the target knows what they’re looking for.

Genius targets something almost no one else sees. They create a category or cause a paradigm shift. I will discuss this at a later point but I am making note of it now.

The War within You

“Greatness recognizes greatness, and is shadowed by it.” ~ Harold Bloom, The Western Canon: The Books and School of the Ages

Greatness must have a home.

A great artist recognizes another’s work.

However, this is not always true.

If you meet some of the great artists they had egos to match.

However, if you choose to carve yourself then you must also focus on the interior.

This means enunciating your strengths and compensating for your weaknesses.

There is also a delicate balance to strike between believing in your vision and pursuing it with all your will to must and getting feedback.

Sometimes faith needs to become momentarily blind to the world surrounding it. This process is only told to be made permanent by the deceitful demagogue and the like.

Art as a primal urge

“We discovered rock art from 68000 years ago, in Indonesia. Shows this was more widespread than we knew, and more ancient than we thought. The urge to create art is probably as hardwired into humanity as anything else.”

Create with All Your Being

All your love…

Emotions are fuel and power that can be directed. Trying to squelch or subject emotions is to try to stop a tsunami in your soul. Instead, ride with it and direct its flow. Do not drown in it.

It’s generally a waste of time to try to change others because the chemical alchemy of transformation can only be triggered from within.

“We’ve been sold this lie that if you haven’t made it by 30, you’re done. It’s a crushing way to live that ignores how we actually grow. Shakespeare and Freud knew better; they saw life as a series of messy, constant transformations. You’re never actually stuck.” ~ Interllectual talk

A lot of later bloomers exist and early bloomers are the exception from the data we have. Be patient.

Prince produced a lot of music. Like a potter producing many pots, Prince produces plenty of pieces of art. A great artist may even have a low hit rate, it seems each individual has a “% high quality rate” but that may or may not be fixed. However, if you can outproduce a more talented person 10x or 1000x times it doesn’t matter. Talent is overrated compared to drive. Drive is not motivated, motivation is fleeting. Drive is waking up and deciding you’re choosing every emotion in the world to fuel you and drive towards the goal. Drive is doing.

To quote the link on Prince “I don’t think there’s anyone as prolific as Prince in terms of just music creation. He had an output like some Mozart type shit, you know? That’s when you’re possessed with a mission from the universe” Produce the music and “find your sound” as Warren Buffet said with respect to the movie Glenn Miller. Pursue your calling so intensely it doesn’t feel like work at all.

This is the goal. Meaning is revealed through hell and back, not in a field of dandelions.

“Your mind will take the shape of what you frequently hold in thought, for the human spirit is colored by such impressions” ~ Marcus Aurelius

An artist is at war with the status quo. At war with themselves to be reshaped by the process.

Real artists ship because they are in a war to become a better version of themselves. It is better to ship than stagnate. It is better to ship than worry. Real artists ship. They ship early, they ship often. They ship with a process finely tuned to the target. You are at war with the resistance.

The Resistance

“Resistance is always lying and always full of shit.” ~Steven Pressfield, The War of Art

If you tell me or yourself all the excuses you have why you can’t do it now or why it’s not the right time.

I want you to know that you are simply full of shit.

That’s the resistance talking. If you lose, the resistance wins.

“The artist committing himself to his calling has volunteered for hell, whether he knows it or not. He will be dining for the duration on a diet of isolation, rejection, self-doubt, despair, ridicule, contempt, and humiliation.” Steven Pressfield, The War of Art

Authentic Artistry Lastly, but most importantly of all is intrinsic motivation. When you are in love you want to show and tell the world. You must accept rejection and misunderstanding as the norm of how you will be received. That must be what you are expressing in your art, love of the craft.

“To labor in the arts for any reason other than love is prostitution.” ~ Steven Pressfield

What kind of love in English? The definition of love is rather tangled with a great many different things in English so we must use another language. “l’amour fou because the French know what’s up.
